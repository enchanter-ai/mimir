## Attack 12 — Extra unknown field added outside signature scope

A new top-level field `x_extra_field` with value `attacker-controlled-data` has been injected into the envelope after signing. Per §15.16 (Forward-Compatibility and Unknown-Field Handling), a Consumer **MUST** compute the canonical form over the **full** received envelope, including unknown fields. Because the Producer signed a canonical form that did not contain `x_extra_field`, the canonical form the Consumer computes (with the extra field) differs from what was signed, and signature verification **MUST** fail (`VE-008`). Verdict: **REJECT**. Note: §15.16 also says a Consumer **MAY** conservatively reject unknown fields with `VE-012` (unknown_field_stripped/present) — both REJECT paths are valid. The primary rejection reason here is `VE-008` because the signature no longer covers the canonical form of the received object. Spec §9.2, §10.2 step 8–11, §15.16.
